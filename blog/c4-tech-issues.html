Week 4 - Designing for Accessibility

2015/6/15

Designing with accessibility in mind is–first and foremost–the right thing to do, but it can also be an interesting design challenge.

How can we design for accessibility? Simple examples include using html5 content tags (header, nav, aside, footer, main, etc.) and alt tags for images to assist screen readers; enabling complete keyboard input for people unable to use a mouse; and providing good contrast for text and between different elements.

Making your code usable on the widest range of devices by the widest range of people you feasibly can should always be your goal, but of course it's an ongoing goal. Here are some resources to help us get started:

First, here is an infographic that lays out many of the basic techniques you can use to increase the accessibility of your site:
http://webaim.org/resources/designers/

If you want to go into a lot more detail, this is a good place to start:
http://www.w3.org/standards/webdesign/accessibility#doit

Here's a really cool article about the technologies making smart phones accessible to blind people (I just didn't know how it worked, so I looked it up):
