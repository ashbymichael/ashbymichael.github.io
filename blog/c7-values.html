Week 7 - Values

2015/7/12

It's important to know what your values are before they're tested. Here are a few of the values I aspire to, and why I think they're worthwhile.

Fairness, I think, is the most important of my values. It is the best, most direct way I know to figure out the difference between right and wrong. It's a basic value, applicable to many different situations, and easily testable by the golden and silver rules.

Teamwork and Independence. At first, they seem like really different values–almost mutually exclusive values. But to me they are related and separately achievable goals, not opposite ends of a spectrum. My ideal working environment is one with a strong sense of teamwork, where every member feels a sense of responsibility to each other and to the team. This is the kind of team where I can be driven not to let my team down, and always know if another team member needs help. The independence aspect is that I want to trust my team to do what they need to do, and I want them to trust me to provide what I need to provide. (It's a very POODR-compatible philosophy.) Earning that trust–earning that independence–is my most important responsibility.

Webster's Dictionary defines integrity as, 1: " the quality of being honest and fair", and 2: "the state of being complete or whole". It's important to be honest with others, and with yourself. And always bring your whole self.

Courage is probably the other most important value. It doesn't matter what other values you have if you don't have the courage to see them through.
