Chefs in the Kitchen

2015/5/24

Coming into Dev Bootcamp, I feel confident in my preparation. I looked up what was on Dev Bootcamp's curriculum (Ruby, Rails, Javascript, SQL) and I started preparing as early as I could, so I feel like I'm off to a good start on that foot.

On the other hand, of course there are aspects that I can't really prepare for–Just how intense is the onsite portion? How will I do when I don't have as much time as I want to study as I want? What do I do when I don't know what to do? But I signed up for a crazy experience, and now I just have to try to be ready for it.

At the top of the video, Shereef mentions the two fear that students commonly have: that they either won't be good enough, or that their partner won't be as good as them. When I started looking into coding bootcamps, I was definitely in the first camp–I don't have a CS background or credentials, and this can be a dense and esoteric field. However, I think I've done an acceptable job of channeling that anxiety into action, and I hope I can keep the momentum.

One final note on the video's main message, regarding kitchen and table mentalities: I've experienced this phenomenon quite a bit from the other side as an EFL/ESL teacher. I had many students who were perfectly intelligent and motivated to learn, but they didn't seem to get how to perform their role as student. I tried reminding them that, however much I might want to, and no matter how much money they paid, I couldn't give them a second language. Ultimately, learning is something you have to do.

Now we'll see if I can apply what I learned as a teacher to my own performance as a student.
